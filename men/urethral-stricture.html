Patient-friendly guide

We provide comprehensive care for common urological conditions affecting men.

What is this?

Urethral stricture is a narrowing of the urethra — the tube that carries urine out of the body. This narrowing makes it difficult for urine to pass freely. It may develop due to an old injury, infection, or previous urological procedure. Many men live with this for years without knowing the real cause of their urinary problems.

Common symptoms

Talk to us if these sound familiar or keep coming back.

  • check_circle Slow, thin, or split urine stream
  • check_circle Straining to urinate
  • check_circle Feeling of incomplete bladder emptying
  • check_circle Recurrent urinary tract infections
  • check_circle Pain or discomfort while urinating

When to seek help

When to worry: If you’re unable to pass urine at all or the stream has become very weak suddenly, seek care immediately.
